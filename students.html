Students: we get ya.

ADHD can be difficult.

One of the most important things for navigating your diagnosis is first understanding how it affects you and knowing what your rights as a student are for accommodations and accessibility. There are two laws that protect and ensure equal access to education and school activities for students with disabilities: the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973. Both are designed to guarantee free and appropriate public education (FAPE) to all children, regardless of ability.

To learn more about your specific rights as a student with ADHD, visit the Department of Education- Office of Civil Liberties website here.

Eye to Eye

Eye to Eye is a national mentoring movement that seeks to pair kids with learning disabilities and attention-deficit hyperactive disorder with similarly labeled college and high school mentors. Using a proven art curriculum, mentors help their mentees build self-esteem and other self-advocacy skills to succeed in school and life, and in doing so develop important leadership and communication skills and grow confidence in their own abilities. The movement currently has 56 local chapters across 20 states, they have most recently released a useful study app, and have improved their social media engagement to provide as many students as possible with the resources they may need.

To find or be a mentor, or to locate your local chapter, click here.

HowToADHD

HowToADHD is a YouTube channel created by Jessica McCabe, a woman who shares her experiences with and knowledge of her own ADHD to provide a resource specifically for individuals who have ADHD. Her page and channel have provided people with ADHD a forum and community to share with and support one another.

If you are interested in learning to understand your own ADHD or find an online forum designed for people with ADHD click here.
